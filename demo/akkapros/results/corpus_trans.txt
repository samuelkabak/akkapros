“Who regulates dike and ditch, who patterns the furrowed ground.
“Enbilulu they shall praise thirdly as (26) ENBILULU-GUGAL, irrigator of the watercourses of the gods,
“Lord of abundance, plenty, high yields,
“Producer of wealth, enricher of the inhabited world,
“Bestower of grain, who causes barley to appear.
“Enbilulu let them speak of fourthly as (27) HEGAL, who heaps up abundance for the peoples,
“Who rains prosperity over the wide earth, who makes vegetation flourish.
“(28) SIRSIR, who heaped up the mountain over Tiamat,
“Who ravaged the corpse of Tiamat with [his] weapon,
“Leader of the land, their faithful shepherd,
“To whom have been granted the cultivated field, the subsistence field, the furrow,
“Who crossed vast Tiamat back and forth in his wrath,
“Passing back and forth, as a bridge, at the place of single combat with her.
“Sirsir they named secondly (29) MALAH, so be it,
“Tiamat is his vessel and he the boatman.
“(30) GIL, who stores up grain in massive mounds,
“Who brings forth barley and flocks, grantor of the land’s seed.
“(31) GILIMMA, who established the bond of the gods, creator of enduring things,
“The bridle that curbed them, provider of good things,
“(32) AGILIMMA, the lofty one, remover of the diadem, who controls the snow,
“Creator of the earth above the waters, establisher of the heights of heaven.
“(33) ZULUM, who assigned fields for the gods, measured off his handiwork,
“Grantor of portions and food offerings, tender of sanctuaries.
“(34) MUMMU, creator of heaven and netherworld, who guides the lost,
“Divine purifier of heaven and netherworld, is, secondly, (35) ZULUMMU,
“To whom no other among the gods was equal in strength.
“(35) GISHNUMUNAB, creator of all peoples, who made the world regions,
“Who destroyed Tiamat’s gods, who made human beings from parts of them.
“(36) LUGALABDUBUR, the king who dissipated the preparations of Tiamat, neutralized [her] weapons,
“Whose support is firm in front and rear.
“(37) PAGALGUENNA, foremost of all lords, whose strength is outstanding,
“Who was greatest of the gods his brethren, lord of them all.
“(38) LUGALDURMAH, king of the bond of the gods, lord of the “Great Bond,”
“Who is greatest in the royal abode, most exalted by far among the gods.
“(39) ARANUNNA, counsellor of Ea, creator of the gods [his] fathers,
“Who, for his lordly ways no god whatever could equal.
“(40) DUMUDUKU, who renews for himself his pure dwelling in “Holy Hill,”
“Son of “Holy Hill,” without whom the lord of “Holy Hill” makes no decision.
“(41) LUGALSHUANNA, king whose strength is outstanding among the gods,
“Lord, strength of Anu, he who became supreme, chosen of Anshar.
“(42) IRUGGA, who ravaged all of them amidst Tiamat,
“Who gathered all wisdom to himself, universal in perception.
“(43) IRQINGU, ravager of Qingu, ... of battle,
“Who took charge of all commands, established lordship.
“(44) KINMA, commander of all the gods, grantor of counsel,
“At whose name the gods quake for fear like a whirlwind.
“(45) DINGIR-ESIZKUR shall dwell aloft in the house of prayer,
“The gods shall bring in their presents before him,
“Until he receives their due.
“No one but he can create artful things,
“The four peoples of this land are his creatures,
“No god but he knows how long they will live.
“(46) GIBIL, who tempered(?) the weapons,
“Who, in the battle with Tiamat, could create artful things,
“Universal in wisdom, ingenious in perception,
“Whose heart is so deep that all the gods together cannot comprehend it.
“(47) ADDU shall be his name, the whole sky he shall cover,
“His beneficent roar shall thunder over the earth,
“As he rumbles, he shall reduce the burden of the clouds, below, for the people, he shall grant sustenance.
“(48) ASHARU, who, according to his name, mustered the gods of destinies,
“He has taken all peoples in his charge.
“(49) NEBERU shall hold the crossing place of heaven and earth,
“They shall not cross above and below without awaiting him,
“Neberu is his star that he made visible in the skies.
“He shall stand at the turning point, they shall look upon him,
“Saying, ‘He who crossed back and forth, without resting, in the middle of Tiamat,
‘Neberu (“Crossing”) shall be his name, who holds the middle of it.’
“He shall fix the motions of the stars of heaven,
“He shall herd all the gods like sheep.
“He shall keep Tiamat subdued, he shall keep her life cut short,
“In the future of humankind, with the passing of time,
“He shall proceed unhindered, he shall persevere till distant days.
“Because he created the firmament and fashioned the surface of the earth,
“Father Enlil has named (him) his own name (50) ‘Lord of the World’.”
When Ea had heard all the names the Igigi-gods had named (him),
He was joyful of heart.
Saying, “He whose name his fathers have glorified,
“His name, like mine, shall be ‘Ea’.
“He shall be master of the sum total of my powers,
“He shall execute of all my commands.”
The great gods named his fifty names
“The Fifty,” they made his position supreme.
They must be grasped: the “first one” should reveal (them),
The wise and knowledgeable should ponder (them) together,
The master should repeat, and make the pupil understand.
The “shepherd,” the “herdsman” should pay attention,
He must not neglect the Enlil of the gods, Marduk,
So his land may prosper and he himself be safe.
His word is truth, his command unalterable,
Not one god can contradict what he says.
If he frowns, he will not relent,
If he is angry, no god can face his rage.
His heart is deep, his feelings all encompassing,
He before whom crime and sin must appear for judgment.
The revelation (of the names) that the “first one” discoursed before him (Marduk),
He wrote down and preserved for the future to hear,
The destiny of Marduk whom the Igigi-gods exalted,
Wherever water is the drink, his name let them invoke.
Let them sound abroad the song of Marduk,
How he defeated Tiamat and took kingship.
